Tasks A–D Straight-and-Level, Climbs, Descents, Turns to Headings
Instrument Cross-Check Scan
Instruments are cross-checked in a continuous pattern. No single instrument gives the full picture — cross-reference all six:
Pitch: AI, altimeter, VSI, ASI
Bank: AI, turn coordinator, HI
Power: ASI, tachometer/MAP, VSI

Common scan errors: Fixating on one instrument. Neglecting the AI. Chasing the altimeter or VSI (which are lagging instruments). The AI gives the most immediate pitch and bank information.
AFH Ch.7
ACS Tolerances — Instrument Flight
Straight-and-level: ±100 ft, ±10 kts, ±10° heading
Constant airspeed climbs/descents: ±100 ft at level off, ±10 kts, ±10° heading
Turns to headings: Rollout within ±10° of target heading, ±100 ft, ±10 kts

Standard rate turn: 3°/second = 360° in 2 minutes. Bank angle ≈ TAS ÷ 10 + 5. At 100 kts: ≈ 15° of bank. The turn coordinator's standard rate mark aligns with standard rate.
